📖 Science Study Notes

⚡ Motion and Forces
MotionChange in position
ForceA push or pull
WindMoving air — a natural force
Natural ForcesInclude wind and moving water
PushA force that moves an object away from the source
PullA force that makes an object move toward the source
RollingType of movement where an object turns over and over
FrictionForce that resists sliding or rolling
CarryingMotion that supports an object while it moves from one place to another
Original PositionThe point where you start before moving
Moving FartherMoving away from an object, increasing your distance from it
Moving CloserMoving toward an object
Things that Affect MotionSize, weight, shape, material, texture of surface
🔊 Sound
SoundComes from vibrations
Loud SoundCreated by strong vibrations
Soft SoundCreated by weak vibrations
VolumeLoudness or softness of sound
PitchHighness or lowness of sound; depends on frequency
High-pitched SoundProduced by fast vibration
Low-pitched SoundProduced by slow vibration
TensionPulling force that stretches an object
TimbreThe quality of sound
Pleasant SoundClear pattern of sound; also called harmonious sound
Unpleasant SoundLoud and difficult to listen to; also called noise
Sound WavesVibrations that transmit sound through a medium to our ears
MediumCan be solid, liquid, or gas — needed for sound to travel
VacuumSound waves CANNOT travel through a vacuum
EchoSound you hear coming back to you after bouncing off a surface
EcholocationAbility to know location and surroundings using sound (used by bats)
UltrasoundSound waves too high to be heard by humans
2D EchoUltrasound procedure that helps doctors understand how the heart is doing
💡 Light
SunNatural source of light; main source on Earth
BioluminescenceAbility of animals to make their own light (e.g., fireflies)
Artificial Source of LightLight made by humans — lamps, flashlights, lightbulbs
How Light BehavesTravels in a straight line · bends · bounces back (reflects)
ShadowDark area formed behind an object that blocks light
Transparent ObjectAllows light to pass through completely (e.g., clear glass)
Translucent ObjectAllows some light to pass through (e.g., frosted glass)
Opaque ObjectDoes not allow any light to pass through; forms a shadow
ReflectionWhen light bounces back after hitting a surface
Uses of LightHelps you see things · used as decoration · used to communicate
