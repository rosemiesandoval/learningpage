📖 Scientists Study Notes

🇵🇭 Filipino Scientists
Arturo P. AlcarazVolcanologist; "Father of Geothermal Energy Development" in the Philippines
Julian A. BanzonBiochemist; researched alternative fuels (coconut/sugarcane ethyl esters) and coconut oil extraction; National Scientist Award
Fe V. del MundoFirst Filipina National Scientist; founded The Children's Medical Center (1957), the first pediatric hospital in the country
Geminiano T. de Ocampo"Father of Modern Philippine Ophthalmology"; established the De Ocampo Eye Hospital
Clara Y. Lim-SyliancoChemist who studied mutagens (harmful substances that damage DNA) and DNA repair mechanisms
Gregorio Y. ZaraEngineer/physicist; "Father of Videoconferencing"; invented the first two-way videophone (1955)
Aisa MijenoEngineer who invented the SALt (Sustainable Alternative Lighting) lamp, a saltwater-powered lantern
Aletta T. YñiguezMarine biologist; develops tools/models for BFAR and DENR; advocates sustainable fishing
🌍 Foreign Scientists
Marie CurieDiscovered radium and polonium; pioneered radiation therapy; developed mobile X-ray units in WWI
Charles Darwin"Father of Evolution"; theory of evolution by natural selection (Beagle voyage)
Isaac NewtonInvented the reflecting telescope; Three Laws of Motion, Law of Universal Gravitation; helped develop calculus
Albert EinsteinFamous for the Theory of Relativity (space, time, gravity, universe)
Ada LovelaceWorld's first computer programmer; worked on Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine
Louis PasteurDeveloped pasteurization, vaccines for anthrax and rabies, and the germ theory of disease
Katie BoumanKey member of the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) project — first image of a black hole
Charles KaoWon 2009 Nobel Prize in Physics for light transmission through optical fibers (fiber-optic communication)
⭐ Traits of a Scientist
Scientific MethodCareful process of observation, experimentation, and analysis
CuriosityDesire to learn and understand new things
Attention to DetailAbility to focus on often overlooked aspects of a task
PatienceImportant when results take months or years to materialize
Good Communication SkillsAbility to convey ideas and findings clearly and concisely
Critical ThinkingAbility to analyze facts and reach reasoned judgments and conclusions
CourageWillingness to tackle important problems in society and seek solutions
PersistenceWorking diligently toward a goal despite challenges; continuing to refine work after research is done
