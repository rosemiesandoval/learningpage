🧱 Physical Properties of Materials
Physical PropertyA characteristic that can be observed or measured
HardnessAbility of a material to resist scratching
BrittlenessTendency of a material to break, crack, or snap easily
MalleabilityEase with which a material can be hammered or rolled into thin sheets
ElasticityAbility of a material to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed
Melting PointTemperature at which a solid changes into a liquid (ice = 0°C)
Boiling PointTemperature at which a liquid turns into a gas (water = 100°C)
Most Malleable MetalGold
🔥 Chemical Properties of Materials
Chemical PropertyCharacteristic observable when a material undergoes a chemical reaction
CombustibilityA material's tendency to ignite and sustain burning
FlammabilityAbility of an object to burn in the presence of oxygen
ReactivityAbility of a material to react with another substance to form something new
CorrosionProcess that produces rust when iron reacts with oxygen
ToxicityDegree to which a material can damage a living organism
BiodegradabilityAbility of an object to decompose due to microorganisms
