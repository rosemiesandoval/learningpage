📚 Chapter Summary: A point is an exact location in space — it has no length or thickness. A line is made up of an infinite number of points and extends forever in both directions. A line segment is part of a line with two definite endpoints. A ray is part of a line that starts at one point (its endpoint) and extends in only one direction forever. An angle is formed when two rays share a common endpoint, called the vertex; the two rays are called the sides of the angle.
TermHow to Name It
📍 PointA capital letter — e.g., point A
↔️ LineDouble arrow over two capital letters — line AB or line BA
📏 Line SegmentA bar over two capital letters — segment AB or BA
➡️ RaySingle arrow over two letters; the first letter is always the endpoint — ray AB
📐 Angle∠ symbol with the vertex, or three letters with vertex in the middle — ∠N or ∠BNC
📌 REMEMBER When naming an angle with three letters, the vertex must always be in the middle — for example, ∠BNC, where N is the vertex, not ∠NBC or ∠CBN.
🧠 Memory Trick "Point has no Length, Line goes both ways, Segment has 2 Ends, Ray goes 1 way." Say it out loud to remember the four basic figures!
📚 Chapter Summary: Parallel lines are lines that do not intersect or meet each other — they always stay the same distance apart. Intersecting lines are lines or line segments that cross each other at one single point, called the point of intersection. Perpendicular lines are a special kind of intersecting lines that meet or cross each other at right angles (90°).
Type of LinesDescriptionExample
🚂 Parallel LinesNever intersect or meetRailroad tracks, opposite sides of a ruler
✖️ Intersecting LinesCross each other at one pointThe letter X, two roads meeting at a crossing
➕ Perpendicular LinesIntersect at right angles (90°)The corner of a book, a plus (+) sign
📌 REMEMBER All perpendicular lines are also intersecting lines, because they cross at a point — but not all intersecting lines are perpendicular, since they don't always cross at a right angle.
🧠 Memory Trick "Parallel — Never Meet. Intersecting — Cross Once. Perpendicular — Cross at a Perfect Corner."
📚 Chapter Summary: The measure of an angle is the amount of opening between its two sides. A protractor is the tool used to measure angles, and the unit of measurement is the degree (°). To measure an angle, place the vertex at the 0-point (center) of the protractor and follow the markings from 0° to the other side of the angle. To draw an angle, use a ruler to draw a straight line, place the protractor's center at one end (the vertex), mark the desired degree, then connect the mark to the vertex with the ruler. Angles are classified as acute, right, or obtuse according to their measure.
Kind of AngleMeasure
🔺 Acute AngleGreater than 0° but less than 90°
⬛ Right AngleExactly 90°
📐 Obtuse AngleGreater than 90° but less than 180°
📌 REMEMBER A protractor is used to measure angles, and its unit is the degree (°). Always line up the vertex with the 0-point (center) of the protractor before reading the measurement.
🧠 Memory Trick "Acute is cute and small (under 90°), Right stands tall like a square corner (exactly 90°), Obtuse is huge and wide (over 90°)."
