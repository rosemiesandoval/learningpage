📚 Chapter Summary: Timbre is the tone quality of sound that makes one instrument or voice sound different from another — often called the "color" of sound. Dynamics are the levels of softness or loudness in music, shown through Italian terms like piano (soft) and forte (loud). Two Philippine ensembles show these concepts well: the kulintangan, a set of bossed gongs from the Islamized regions of Mindanao, and the tultugan, a group of bamboo instruments popular in the Visayas. Vocal timbre makes each singer's voice unique, while vocal range is the scope of pitches a singer can produce, grouped into soprano, mezzo-soprano, alto (female) and tenor, baritone, bass (male).

🥁 Kulintangan (Mindanao)Kulintang (melody), Agung (deep sound), Dabakan (rhythm), Gandingan (drone), Babandil (timekeeper)
🎋 Tultugan (Visayas)Tultug (timekeeper), Gabbang (melody), Bamboo Pipes (melody), Bayong (bass), Tala-tala (cymbals)
📌 REMEMBER The kulintangan ensemble is usually played forte (loud) since it's used at festivities and celebrations. Both ensembles are percussive — their instruments are struck, shaken, or scratched to produce sound.
🧠 Memory Trick "Piano is quiet, Forte is loud — the more letters, the more it's allowed!" pp is softer than p, and ff is louder than f.
📚 Chapter Summary: Folk dance is rooted in the traditions and cultural practices of a community, often telling a story or reflecting daily life. Philippine folk dances are grouped as ethnic (precolonial) or localized (mixed with foreign influence). The polka, from Bohemia, is set in duple meter with a "step-close-step" pattern; a popular variety is polka sa nayon from Batangas. The pandanggo, adapted from the Spanish fandango, is set in triple meter; its most famous variety, pandanggo sa ilaw from Mindoro, features dancers balancing lit oil lamps called tinghoy.
DanceOriginMeterKey Feature
💃 PolkaBohemia (Czechia)DupleStep-close-step pattern
🕺 Polka sa NayonBatangasDupleDanced in pairs at fiestas
🕯️ PandanggoAdapted from Spanish fandangoTripleAccompanied by guitar/banduria
🏮 Pandanggo sa IlawMindoroTripleBalancing tinghoy oil lamps
📌 REMEMBER Polka = duple meter, from Europe. Pandanggo = triple meter, from Spain. Both became localized Filipino folk dances after being blended with local elements.
🧠 Memory Trick "Polka steps in twos (duple), Pandanggo sways in threes (triple)." Count the beat to remember the meter!
📚 Chapter Summary: According to the WHO, health is a condition of total physical, mental, and social well-being — not just the absence of illness. Wellness means having good physical condition through a healthy lifestyle and a positive outlook. Personal health is the ability to actively choose healthy living. Overall well-being is influenced by five dimensions of health: physical, mental, emotional, social, and spiritual. Practicing good personal hygiene helps prevent illness and builds confidence and self-esteem.
DimensionDescriptionExample Activity
💪 Physical HealthCondition of the body's size, structure, organs, and functionsExercise, good hygiene, healthy diet, enough sleep
🧠 Mental HealthAbility to think, understand, and process informationDoing homework, solving problems, answering tests
😊 Emotional HealthAbility to show and control feelingsSmiling at compliments, controlling anger
🤝 Social HealthAbility to adjust to situations and interact with othersPlaying outdoor games, communicating, keeping commitments
🙏 Spiritual HealthFaith and beliefs in seeking a purpose in lifePraying, visiting places of worship, having inner peace
📌 REMEMBER The two main goals of personal health are to maintain good physical health and to keep one's environment germ free. Good hygiene projects a positive body image and boosts self-esteem.
🧠 Memory Trick "P-M-E-S-S: Physical, Mental, Emotional, Social, Spiritual" — the five dimensions that keep you well and balanced!
